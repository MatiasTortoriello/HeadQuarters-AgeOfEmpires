Age of Empires II is a real-time strategy video game developed by Ensemble Studios and published by Microsoft. Released in 1999 for Microsoft Windows and Macintosh, it is the second game in the Age of Empires series. The game is set in the Middle Ages and contains 39 playable civilizations. Players aim to gather resources, which they use to build towns, create armies, and defeat their enemies. There are many historically based campaigns, which constrict the player to specialized and story-backed conditions, as well as a variety of additional single and multiplayer game modes.

  • The Age of Kings

    The first game was launched on 1999, contained 13 civilizations and five campaings (William Wallace, Joan of Arc, Barbarrossa, Ghenghis Khan and Saladin). Its reception was highly positive. Three months after its release, two million copies of The Age of Kings had been shipped. The game won multiple awards and is today considered a classic of its type, having had a significant impact on future games in its genre.

  • The Conquerors

    First expansion, The Conquerors, was released in 2000. It featured five new civilizations (the Aztecs, Mayans, Spanish, Koreans, and Huns), four new campaigns (El Cid, Attila, Montezuma and Battles of the Conquerors), eleven new units, twenty-six new technologies, new gameplay modes, new maps and different minor tweaks to the gameplay.

  • Forgotten Empires (HD)

    The pack introduces five new civilizations: the Incas, Indians, Italians, Magyars, and Slavs. It includes new maps, campaigns (Alaric I, Bari, Dracula, El Dorado, Francesco I Sforza, and Prithviraj), units, a new game mode, and numerous balance and gameplay adjustments.

  • African Kingdoms

    The African Kingdoms, was released on November 5, 2015, and introduces four new civilizations: the Berbers, Ethiopians, Malians, and the Portuguese. It includes new maps, campaigns (Francisco de Almeida, Sundjata, Tariq ibn Ziyad, and Yodit), and units, new game modes including Capture the Relic and Treaty, and numerous balance and gameplay adjustments.

  • Rise of The Rajas

    Rise of the Rajas, was released on December 19, 2016. It is set in Southeast Asia, and adds four civilizations (Burmese, Khmer, Malay, and Vietnamese), each with its own fully voice-acted campaign (Bayinnaung, Gajah Mada, Lê Lợi, and Suryavarman I), as well as a new map type with environments, units, improved AI, and more.

  • The Last Khans (DE)

    The remaster, Age of Empires 2: Definitive Edition, included the Last Khans expansion. It included all content from the previous editions and expansions, as well as four new civilizations (Bulgarians, Cumans, Lithuanians, and Tatars), additional campaign missions (Ivaylo, Kotyan Khan, Pachacuti, and Tamerlane), and new 4K graphics.

  • Lords of the West

    This expansion features two new civilizations – the Burgundians and the Sicilians – as well as three new campaigns (Edward I Longshanks, Grand Dukes of Burgundy, and The Hautevilles). The new campaigns consist of one for each new civilization, as well as a new campaign for the existing Britons civilization.

  • Dawn of the Dukes

    The expansion's focus is on Eastern Europe and includes two new civilizations (Poles and Bohemians), two new campaigns based on the aforementioned new civilizations (Jadwiga and Jan Žižka) and a campaign for the existing Lithuanian civilization (Algirdas and Kęstutis).
